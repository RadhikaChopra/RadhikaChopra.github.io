My ideal job: Learning Designer

The position: The position is for a learning designer. Someone who can help build online courses for universities using best practice in coding, and knowledge of teaching pedagogy and curriculum building. This is appealing to me as I am a teacher at the moment and I love being in the classroom, but I would like the opportunity to work on developing resources and online material. The position would allow me to put my love of education together with my interest in coding and design.

Skills and qualifications: For this job the main skills would be knowing how to code in various languages used in learning management systems, knowledge of design principles with attention to detail, and an idea of University style education. You would also need strong communication skills, ability to work in a team, and deal with clients. You would possibly need a qualification as a teacher (VIT) or TAE certificate. You may or may not require a qualification in IT or design.

My current skills: I have two degrees (Journalism and Visual Art) and have completed a CELTA certificate, which allows me to teach English as a second language. I have worked within private institutes, University and TAFE settings as an ELICOS/EAP teacher for the past three years. I have experience building curriculums for courses both in the classroom and online. I am familiar with learning management systems such as Moodle and Canvas, and have used them in both a student and teacher capacity. I have past experience in coding in HTML and CSS from my degrees and jobs, as well as my own personal projects. I also have a strong personal interest in education and technology.

My plan: My plan is to finish my course in Information technology, and build on my coding skills in my own time. I will then apply for junior positions or internships to get hands-on experience in the role. I will also apply through my current employer and see if I can gain some training with our learning design team as part of my professional development.
